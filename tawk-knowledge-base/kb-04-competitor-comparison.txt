DELIVER WMS — KNOWLEDGE BASE FILE 04: COMPETITOR COMPARISON
===========================================================

Honest comparison of Deliver WMS against the five WMS platforms most often
encountered in evaluation: Extensiv, Logiwa, ShipHero, CartonCloud, and
Fishbowl. Full content at https://deliverwms.com/compare.

================================================================
FRAMING
================================================================

Every platform listed below has happy customers. Different choices for
different operations. We're describing how each product approaches the
problem — not arguing anyone is bad. Pick the one whose architecture matches
the way you want to run.

================================================================
DIMENSION 1: TENANT MODEL
================================================================

DELIVER WMS: Multi-tenant from day one. Every table, query, and policy is
tenant-aware. Built specifically for warehouses serving multiple clients.

EXTENSIV: Multi-tenant native. Strong choice for established 3PLs.

LOGIWA: Multi-tenant native. Heavier feature footprint than Deliver WMS today.

SHIPHERO: Historically focused on single-brand merchants. Adding 3PL features.

CARTONCLOUD: Multi-tenant native, with regional strength in APAC.

FISHBOWL: Single-brand QuickBooks-aligned. Not designed for 3PL multi-tenancy.

WHY THIS MATTERS: We started with multi-tenancy because retrofitting it into
a single-brand WMS is structurally hard. Tables, queries, and policies are
all tenant-aware from day one — that's why a 3PL serving 20 clients sees
clean data isolation, not a bolted-on filter.

================================================================
DIMENSION 2: BILLING & FINANCIAL TRUTH
================================================================

DELIVER WMS: Built on a transparent ledger. Every billable event captured at
the moment it occurs — invoices reflect what actually happened, with full
audit trail.

EXTENSIV: Configurable billing rules; reconciliation typical of legacy WMS.

LOGIWA: Billing module available; depends on third-party export for finance
ops.

SHIPHERO: Per-action fees with monthly billing summary.

CARTONCLOUD: Built-in invoicing; some manual reconciliation typical.

FISHBOWL: Tightly tied to QuickBooks; billing flows through accounting.

WHY THIS MATTERS: Most legacy WMS bolt billing onto operational data later.
We chose to capture the billable event at the moment of action because that's
the only way the invoice can be defended in front of a customer, line by line,
without reconstructing it from logs at month end.

================================================================
DIMENSION 3: AUTOMATION
================================================================

DELIVER WMS: No-code rules engine. Operators wire automations without
engineering support — event stream feeds rules; rules trigger actions.

EXTENSIV: Workflow configuration available; complex rules typically need
professional services.

LOGIWA: Workflow rules engine; broader feature set.

SHIPHERO: Automation rules available, mostly merchant-facing.

CARTONCLOUD: Automation features included; varies by plan.

FISHBOWL: Limited automation; workflow rules are basic.

WHY THIS MATTERS: Operators know their workflows better than any
implementation consultant. We built a plain-English rules engine on top of
the event stream so the people running the warehouse can wire automations
themselves — without filing a ticket or waiting on a services engagement.

================================================================
DIMENSION 4: MIGRATION & ONBOARDING
================================================================

DELIVER WMS: CSV import with smart field mapping. Most mid-size 3PLs
operational on Deliver WMS within a week.

EXTENSIV: White-glove implementation included; thorough but slower.

LOGIWA: Implementation services available; multi-week typical.

SHIPHERO: Self-serve onboarding for merchants; 3PL setup is consultative.

CARTONCLOUD: Implementation typically supported by their team.

FISHBOWL: Self-installed; depends heavily on QuickBooks setup.

WHY THIS MATTERS: Long implementations are how WMS deals die. We built CSV
import with smart field mapping so an operator can bring their existing data
into Deliver WMS without an SOW or a six-week consulting engagement — the
trade-off is that white-glove migration is your call to opt into, not the
default.

================================================================
DIMENSION 5: MARKETPLACE INTEGRATIONS
================================================================

DELIVER WMS: On the roadmap. Order-import API and webhook events available
today for custom connections.

EXTENSIV: Established integrations with major marketplaces.

LOGIWA: Native Shopify, Amazon integrations available.

SHIPHERO: Strongest in this dimension — built integrations-first for DTC
merchants.

CARTONCLOUD: Marketplace integrations available; varies by region.

FISHBOWL: Marketplace integrations weaker than competitors; QuickBooks-first.

WHY THIS MATTERS: We're honest about this one. Native marketplace connectors
are on the roadmap, not shipped. The order-import API and typed webhook
events are usable today for custom integrations, and we'd rather tell you
that than pretend a connector exists. ShipHero is genuinely strong here if
marketplace breadth is your top priority.

================================================================
WHEN TO CHOOSE DELIVER WMS
================================================================

Deliver WMS is the right choice when:

  - You run a multi-client 3PL (3-10 active client brands) at mid-size
    volume (200-1,000 monthly outbound orders) and want data isolation
    enforced at the database level
  - You want pricing you can see before a sales call
  - Event-stream billing matters to you (defensible invoices, faster close,
    fewer disputes)
  - You want to be operational in a week, not six
  - You don't want to pay per user
  - You value an opinionated product over a fully-configurable one
  - You want a vendor where the founder writes the code and talks to
    operators directly

Deliver WMS is probably NOT the right choice when:

  - You need native Shopify/Amazon/WooCommerce integrations today (those are
    on our roadmap; ShipHero is a stronger fit if that's your top priority)
  - You're running 50,000+ monthly orders and need an enterprise WMS with
    dedicated implementation managers (we serve that volume but the
    relationship shape is different from what enterprises typically expect)
  - You require SOC 2 Type II certification today as a hard requirement
    (we're working toward it; not certified yet)
  - You need extensive APAC regional features (CartonCloud has stronger
    regional fit there)

COMPARISON QUICK-REFERENCE FOR CHAT
================================================================

If asked "How do you compare to Extensiv?":
"Extensiv is a strong choice for established 3PLs and has the longest
operating history. We're newer, smaller, and built specifically for mid-size
3PLs that want event-stream billing, published pricing, and a one-week
implementation instead of a six-week consulting engagement. If you're already
on Extensiv and it's working, switching isn't urgent. If you're evaluating
both, the savings calculator at https://deliverwms.com/pricing will give you
a sense of operational time savings on your specific numbers."

If asked "How do you compare to ShipHero?":
"ShipHero is strong in DTC marketplace integrations — they built
integrations-first for that customer. We don't have native marketplace
connectors yet (they're on our roadmap; API/webhook events available today
for custom connections). On the multi-tenant 3PL architecture, billing
ledger, and pricing transparency, we're a more focused fit. If marketplace
integration breadth is your top priority today, ShipHero is the honest
recommendation."

If asked "How do you compare to Logiwa?":
"Logiwa has a heavier feature footprint than us today — that's a real
trade-off. We're more focused, our pricing is transparent, and we ship every
two weeks. For operators who want a leaner product with clearer pricing and
faster iteration on operator-driven priorities, we're a fit. For operators
who need every feature checkbox today, Logiwa is more comprehensive."

If asked "How do you compare to CartonCloud?":
"CartonCloud has regional strength in APAC. If you're in that market, they
deserve a serious look. For US-based 3PLs, our pricing is published openly,
our billing ledger is event-stream rather than reconciled, and our migration
path is a one-week CSV import rather than a multi-week supported engagement."

If asked "How do you compare to Fishbowl?":
"Fishbowl is tightly tied to QuickBooks and built around single-brand
operations. It's a different product shape entirely. If you're running a 3PL
with multiple client brands, you'll likely outgrow Fishbowl's tenant model.
If you're a single-brand operation deeply integrated with QuickBooks,
Fishbowl is closer to your existing workflow than we are."

THE FIVE ARCHITECTURAL REASONS (HEADLINE NARRATIVE)
================================================================

When asked "what's actually different about Deliver WMS?", these five
architectural choices summarize the answer:

1. MULTI-TENANT FROM DAY ZERO. Built with tenant isolation enforced at the
   database, not the application layer. Mathematically prevents one client's
   data from leaking into another's view.

2. EVENT-STREAM BILLING LEDGER. Every billable event captured at the moment
   of action. Invoices defensible line-by-line. Faster close, fewer disputes.

3. NO-CODE AUTOMATION RULES ENGINE. Operators wire automations themselves
   without engineering or consulting support.

4. ONE-WEEK CSV MIGRATION. No SOW. No consulting engagement. Smart field
   mapping handles non-matching column names automatically.

5. PUBLISHED PRICING. All four tiers and per-event rates on a public page.
   No "Contact us" gate.
