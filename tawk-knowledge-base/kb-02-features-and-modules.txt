DELIVER WMS — KNOWLEDGE BASE FILE 02: FEATURES AND MODULES
==========================================================

The Deliver WMS platform has 35 modules organized across 7 categories.
Each module is documented at https://deliverwms.com/docs/<slug>.

This file lists every module with what it does, who uses it, why it matters,
and its full capability list.

================================================================
CATEGORY 1: WAREHOUSE OPERATIONS (9 modules)
================================================================

MODULE: Operations Dashboard
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/dashboard
Tagline: Real-time KPIs, volume trends, and the new-tenant completion checklist.
Audiences: 3PL, eCom, Dist, L-Mile
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner, Warehouse Manager
Why it matters: Operations managers can't afford to dig through five screens
before morning standup. The dashboard gives you the pulse of your entire
operation — orders behind SLA, inbound arrivals due, and floor utilization
— so you know where to focus before your first coffee is cold. For new
tenants, a completion checklist surfaces the four onboarding steps that take
you from signup to operational.
Capabilities: Real-time KPI cards. Volume trend charts. Task completion
tracking. Role-adaptive content. New-tenant completion checklist. Quick-action
shortcuts.

MODULE: Inbound Receiving
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/inbound
Tagline: Receive shipments with a 6-state pallet lifecycle, scan items, handle
exceptions, and trigger putaway.
Audiences: 3PL, eCom, Dist
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner, Warehouse Manager, Warehouse Employee
Why it matters: For 3PLs handling multiple clients, accurate receiving is the
foundation of trust. Every miscount erodes margin and client confidence.
Scan-driven receiving with a fully-tracked pallet lifecycle catches problems
at the dock door — not weeks later during a cycle count. Every pallet has a
place in the lifecycle and a timestamp at every transition.
How it works: Each pallet moves through a six-state lifecycle — expected,
arrived, receiving, putaway, emptied, removed. Every transition is timestamped.
A daily integrity check verifies no pallet has drifted into an inconsistent
state. Operators can scan barcodes to identify items or use manual SKU entry.
The system automatically resolves barcodes against the product catalog and
raises exceptions for unknown items, quantity mismatches, or damaged goods.
Each scan is recorded as an immutable receiving event. Once receiving is
complete, the system generates putaway tasks that direct workers to move
pallets from staging to assigned storage based on zone affinity, client
preferences, and capacity.
Capabilities: Shipment scheduling and dock assignment. Barcode scan and manual
SKU modes. 6-state pallet lifecycle with full timestamping. Exception handling
(damage, shortage, unknown). Pallet-to-inventory junction for cross-checks.
Automatic putaway task generation. Receiving session audit trail.

MODULE: Storage Management
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/storage
Tagline: Monitor zone and rack utilization, manage storage locations, and
optimize space.
Audiences: 3PL, Dist
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner, Warehouse Manager
Why it matters: Storage costs are your biggest fixed expense. Knowing which
zones are underutilized and which clients are overflowing lets you optimize
space allocation before you need to lease a second building. For 3PLs, proper
client-zone affinity prevents cross-contamination and simplifies billing.
Capabilities: Zone and rack utilization heatmaps. Client-to-zone affinity
tracking. Fragmentation scoring. Capacity planning views. Location-level
drill-down.

MODULE: Inventory Management
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/inventory
Tagline: Track SKU-level stock with a ledger-backed audit trail and daily
3-way reconciliation.
Audiences: 3PL, eCom, Dist
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner, Warehouse Manager, B2B Client
Why it matters: Inventory accuracy is the difference between profitable
fulfillment and costly chargebacks. The ledger-backed model means every unit
is accounted for — every receive, every pick, every adjustment — creating an
immutable record that clients and auditors can trust. A daily 3-way
reconciliation confirms the movement history, per-item quantities, and per-bin
balances all agree.
How it works: All quantity changes flow through an append-only inventory
ledger. Every night a 3-way reconciliation confirms the immutable movement
history, the per-item quantity, and the per-bin balance all agree. Any drift
is surfaced as an exception the next morning, not weeks later. A one-click
on-demand self-test lets managers run the same reconciliation any time —
useful before a month-end close or after a large adjustment.
Capabilities: SKU-level stock tracking. Multi-client filtering. Location-based
views. Transfer and adjustment workflows. Low-stock alerts. Append-only ledger
with full audit trail. Daily 3-way reconciliation with exception detection.
On-demand integrity self-test.

URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/tasks
Tagline: Centralized task queue for picks, putaways, counts, and custom

Audiences: 3PL, eCom, Dist
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner, Warehouse Manager, Warehouse Employee
Why it matters: Floor productivity directly impacts your cost-per-order. The

Capabilities: Unified task queue (picks, putaways, counts, custom). Auto-

MODULE: Count Variance Review
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/variance-review
Tagline: Review cycle-count discrepancies, gate adjustments through manager
approval, and post to the append-only ledger.
Audiences: 3PL, Dist
Roles: Platform Owner, Warehouse Manager
Why it matters: Unresolved variances snowball into shrinkage, mis-ships, and
client disputes. A structured review queue with a manager approval gate
ensures every discrepancy gets a decision — approve, reject, or recount —
with a full audit trail. For 3PLs, this is your defense against inventory
claims.
How it works: When a counted quantity differs from the expected system
quantity, it creates a variance record that requires managerial review before
any ledger adjustment can post. Managers approve adjustments (which post to
the inventory ledger as an explicit additional entry, never an overwrite),
reject them (triggering a recount), or add notes for investigation. Every
decision is recorded with timestamps, user attribution, and reason codes for
full audit compliance. The approval gate is the discipline that prevents
silent inventory corrections.
Capabilities: Pending variance queue with severity sorting. Per-bin recount
workflow. Reject / recount / approve gate. Append-only adjustment audit trail.
Reason code categorization. Per-decision actor and timestamp record.

MODULE: Packaging Management
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/packaging
Tagline: Smart box catalog with on-hand counts, cost tracking, and packing
recommendations.
Audiences: 3PL, eCom
Roles: Platform Owner, Warehouse Manager, Packer
Why it matters: Dimensional weight charges from carriers can quietly inflate
shipping costs by 20-30%. The smart packaging engine picks the smallest box
that safely fits — every time, on every order — so you stop paying for shipped
air. Packers don't need to memorize a box chart; the system already knows.
Capabilities: Box and material catalog. On-hand inventory of packaging.
Cost-per-pack tracking. Recommended box sizing. Reorder alerts.

MODULE: Warehouse Settings
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/warehouse-settings
Tagline: Per-warehouse configuration: facility profile, zones, racks, and
operational preferences.
Audiences: 3PL, Dist
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner, Warehouse Manager
Why it matters: The system's intelligence is only as good as your warehouse
model. Spending an hour up-front to define zones, racks, and client affinity
rules means putaway tasks route correctly, storage allocation respects client
preferences, and picks follow efficient paths. Skipping this step turns smart
automation into chaos.
How it works: The General tab handles the facility profile — name, contact,

billing summary surfaces the current tenant plan with a direct link to Account
Management. The Zones and Racks tabs define the physical structure: zone
layouts, rack-bay-level hierarchy, dock door assignments, capacity defaults,
and client affinity rules. Multi-warehouse tenants can configure each location
independently. Team management lives in its own Employees screen; the Settings
to Users entry redirects there with context.
Capabilities: Facility profile (name, contact, phone, address) with full
persistence. Read-only billing summary with deep link to Account Management.
Zone, rack, bay, and level layout. Client-to-zone affinity rules. Operational
hour configuration. Multi-warehouse tenant support.

MODULE: Pallet Lifecycle Health
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/pallet-lifecycle-health
Tagline: Platform-owner integrity dashboard for the inbound pallet lifecycle
with daily exception detection.
Audiences: 3PL
Roles: Platform Owner
Why it matters: If pallets drift between lifecycle states without their

Capabilities: Eleven independent lifecycle assertions. Daily automatic

to underlying records. Mark-removed action with mandatory reason capture.

MODULE: Order Management
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/orders
Tagline: Create, allocate, pick, pack, and ship customer orders with full
lifecycle tracking.
Audiences: 3PL, eCom, Dist
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner, Warehouse Manager, B2B Client
Why it matters: eCommerce brands need sub-24hr fulfillment. The allocation
engine reserves stock the moment an order lands, preventing overselling. For
3PLs managing multiple clients, order isolation ensures one client's rush
orders don't cannibalize another's inventory. Smart routing eliminates the
daily question of "which orders go on trucks vs. carrier?" — the system
decides automatically.
How it works: Order Management handles the complete lifecycle of customer
orders — from creation through allocation, picking, packing, shipping, and
delivery. It supports both manual order entry and integration-sourced orders.
The allocation engine checks available inventory and routes orders to the
appropriate warehouse and carrier flow automatically. Once allocated, the
system generates pick tasks and pushes them into the Task Center for floor
workers. Every status change — created, allocated, picked, packed, shipped,
delivered — is captured as an event with timestamp and user attribution.
Capabilities: Order intake (manual, API, CSV). Allocation and reservation.
Pick / pack / ship workflows. Carrier label generation. Order lifecycle audit
trail.

MODULE: Returns Dashboard
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/returns
Tagline: Process returns, inspect items, set disposition, and update inventory
and billing in one step.
Audiences: 3PL, eCom
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner, Warehouse Manager
Why it matters: Returns are expensive — but untracked returns are devastating.
Disposition-aware processing ensures restockable items get back to available
inventory fast, while damaged goods are quarantined before they ship again.
Reason-code analytics help identify root causes like packaging issues or
wrong-item picks.
Capabilities: Returns intake with photo capture. Per-item disposition
(restock, damage, write-off). Automatic ledger updates. Client-visible status
with timestamps. Billable disposition events.

MODULE: Auto-Allocation Engine
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/auto-allocate
Tagline: One-click inventory allocation that respects multi-warehouse rules
and client affinity.
Audiences: 3PL, eCom, Dist
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner, Warehouse Manager
Why it matters: Manual allocation is slow, error-prone, and doesn't scale.
The auto-allocation engine turns a multi-step process into one click — verify
stock, reserve it, and generate picks — all in a single atomic operation. For
high-volume warehouses, this eliminates the allocation bottleneck that delays
fulfillment start times.
How it works: The allocation process verifies available quantity (on-hand
minus already-reserved) before committing. If all lines can be fully
allocated, the order advances to picking. If not, partial-order splitting
kicks in to peel off the fulfillable lines while backordering the rest.
Multi-warehouse logic ranks locations by distance and stock depth, splitting
across sites only when no single warehouse can fill the order.
Capabilities: Distance-ranked warehouse allocation. Haversine geo math for
stop assignment. Multi-warehouse split-shipment fallback. Primary-warehouse-
wins tie-break. Stockout event logging.

MODULE: Partial Order Splitting
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/partial-order-splitting
Tagline: Automatically splits orders when inventory is spread across multiple
warehouses.
Audiences: 3PL, eCom, Dist
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner, Warehouse Manager
Why it matters: A single out-of-stock SKU shouldn't hold up an entire order.
Partial splitting means you ship what you can today and backorder the rest —
maximizing fulfillment velocity and customer satisfaction. The linked badge
keeps operators aware of the relationship so nothing falls through the cracks.
Capabilities: Cross-warehouse split detection. Reservation per split shipment.
Consolidated tracking under parent order. Per-split label generation.
Customer-friendly tracking page.

Audiences: 3PL
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner
Why it matters: For 3PLs, billing transparency prevents disputes and builds
long-term client relationships. Every billable event is captured the moment

rate cards use a versioned-row pattern: rate changes create new rows, the
previous rate is preserved with its effective-to timestamp, and invoices
generated from historical events use the rate that was in effect at the
moment of the event.
Capabilities: Per-client rate card management with versioned history. Full

MODULE: Employee Management
URL: https://deliverwms.com/docs/employees
Tagline: Invitations, role-aware welcome flow, and team management across the
platform.
Audiences: 3PL, eCom, Dist, L-Mile
Roles: Platform Owner, Business Owner, Warehouse Manager
Why it matters: Role-based access isn't just about security — it's about
focus. Warehouse workers see their task queue, not financial reports. Drivers
see their stops, not inventory ledgers. Proper role assignment reduces errors
and training time. The invitation system + role-aware welcome page makes
new-hire onboarding a one-screen experience.
How it works: Invitations are sent from this screen with rate-limited
resends, hashed tokens, and lifecycle tracking (pending, accepted, expired,
revoked). New invitees receive a single-screen welcome page tailored to their
role. A business owner lands on a workspace orientation, a warehouse employee
on a worker-app intro, a B2B client on a portal walkthrough. Role-appropriate
next-step CTAs drive each invitee to the screen they should use first.
Capabilities: Employee records and roles. RBAC permission assignment.
Invitation system with hashed tokens + rate-limited resends. Role-aware
welcome page for new invitees. Per-invitation lifecycle tracking. Mobile-app
onboarding. Activity tracking. Multi-warehouse assignment.

================================================================
TEN ROLES SUPPORTED ACROSS THE PLATFORM
================================================================

PLATFORM OWNER — Full system administrator with access to all tenants and
configuration. Sees the platform-wide revenue dashboard, can impersonate any
tenant for support, manages tenant lifecycle.

BUSINESS OWNER — Manages business operations, billing, and high-level
reporting. The default role for the operator running a 3PL on the platform.

WAREHOUSE MANAGER — Oversees warehouse floor operations, staff, and inventory
accuracy. Approves variance adjustments, reviews dashboards, manages task
assignments.

WAREHOUSE EMPLOYEE — Executes picks, putaways, counts, and other floor tasks.
Primary user of the Worker App.

PACKER — Packs confirmed picks into shipment-ready parcels. Uses the Worker
App's pack workflow with integrated label generation.

B2B CLIENT — External client of the 3PL with self-service portal access to
inventory, orders, products, billing, and reports. All data scoped strictly
to their own business via database-level row-level security.

END CUSTOMER — Tracks order delivery status via the public Tracking Portal.
No login required.

DRIVER — Delivers orders and confirms proof-of-delivery via the mobile Driver
App. Sees only their own assigned stops.

DISPATCHER — Assigns drivers to routes and monitors delivery progress in real
time via the Dispatcher Console.

SHIPPING MANAGER — Manages outbound logistics, fleet, and delivery scheduling.
Oversees the Dispatch Queue and Fleet screens.
