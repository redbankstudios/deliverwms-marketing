================================================================
COMPANY POSITIONING
================================================================

Deliver WMS LLC is a Delaware-formed software company building a warehouse
management and last-mile delivery platform for mid-size 3PLs, eCommerce
brands, distributors, and last-mile delivery companies.

The platform replaces the patchwork of spreadsheets, legacy WMS tools, and
disconnected delivery apps that slow logistics teams down. The product is
opinionated, transparent about pricing, and ships substantive updates every
couple of weeks.

NATURAL FIT: Mid-size 3PLs running 200 to 1,000 monthly outbound orders for
3 to 10 active client brands. Operations outside that band can use the
platform, but value is sharpest there.

REMOTE-FIRST TEAM serving logistics operations worldwide.

================================================================
THE FOUNDER
================================================================

MICHAEL is the founder of Deliver WMS — a working business owner and
software engineer. Not a sales engineer. He runs the business, writes the
code, talks to operators on the warehouse floor, and sits with the
dispatcher when the route board is on fire at 7am.

Why he built it:

Michael kept watching the same thing happen to small and mid-size warehouse
operators: they grew, the workload grew, and the tools they were sold by
legacy WMS vendors quietly became the bottleneck instead of the leverage.

The spreadsheets started as a workaround. The email threads with clients
started as a courtesy. The month-end billing reconciliation started as a
"we'll fix it later." None of it scaled. And the alternative — six-figure
enterprise WMS quotes with twelve-week implementations — wasn't an
alternative at all for the operators he cared about. So he started building.

Three principles that drive product decisions:

1. SIMPLER BEATS FEATURE-RICH. Most WMS platforms read like enterprise
   checklists. Deliver WMS chooses the smallest interface that lets an
   operator finish the job — then stops adding things.

2. INTUITIVE ENOUGH FOR THE WAREHOUSE FLOOR. If a Day-One picker can't use
   it without a training video, it's broken. Every screen is designed for
   the person actually scanning the carton, not the executive demoing the
   dashboard.

3. PRICED LIKE SOFTWARE, NOT CONSULTING. Legacy WMS pricing punishes you
   for growing. Deliver WMS pricing scales with the value you get — not
   with seats, modules, or implementation hours.

================================================================
SIX ARCHITECTURAL DECISIONS YOU'LL FEEL
================================================================

These six choices separate Deliver WMS from generic warehouse tools.

1. LEDGER-BACKED INVENTORY
Not just CRUD — every movement is an immutable event. Balances are derived
from the ledger, giving an audit trail that survives any dispute.

2. MULTI-TENANT ARCHITECTURE
Tenant isolation isn't bolted on. Every table, every query, every row-level
security policy is tenant-aware from the ground up — so your clients' data
never crosses.

3. WAREHOUSE TO DOORSTEP
No separate WMS, TMS, and delivery app. One platform covers receiving,
storage, fulfillment, dispatch, and last-mile — with shared data across
every step.

4. 10 ROLES, NOT ONE-SIZE-FITS-ALL
From platform owners to floor workers to B2B clients — each role sees
exactly what they need. Role-based access reduces errors and training time.

5. AUTOMATED CARRIER SHIPPING
Orders outside your delivery zones are automatically routed to carriers.
Labels are generated via rate shopping during picking and ready to print
at packing — no manual carrier management.

6. SCAN-DRIVEN RECEIVING
Barcode scanning with automatic exception handling for unknown items,
quantity mismatches, and damage. Unknown barcodes are learned and resolved
for future scans.

================================================================
FROM DOCK TO DOORSTEP — THE 5-STEP FLOW
================================================================

01 RECEIVE & STORE
Inbound shipments are received with barcode scanning, validated against
purchase orders, and directed to optimal storage locations.

02 MANAGE INVENTORY
Track every unit by SKU, location, and client. Ledger-based balances keep
on-hand, reserved, and available quantities accurate.

03 FULFILL ORDERS
Orders are allocated, pick tasks are generated and assigned, items are
packed, carrier labels are auto-generated, and shipments are created with
tracking numbers.

04 DISPATCH & DELIVER
Orders ship via your fleet or carrier — automatically routed by delivery
zone. Plan routes, assign drivers, and track delivery progress in real
time with proof of delivery.

05 REPORT & BILL
Operational dashboards show throughput, SLA performance, and cost drivers.
Billing views give clients full transparency.

TWO COMMITMENTS THAT SHOW UP IN EVERY PRODUCT DECISION
================================================================

HONEST UI. If a tab is decorative, hide it. If a button doesn't do what it
looks like it does, take it off the screen. The Settings page is a recent
example — fields that didn't actually save anywhere were removed, not
"improved with a tooltip."

OPERATOR VOICE. Marketing copy doesn't use SaaS buzzwords ("industry-
leading," "best-in-class," "cutting-edge"). The Compare page openly admits
where competitors are stronger. The blog has a clear operator-to-operator
voice. This is the moat that vendors who employ a marketing team can't
easily copy.
