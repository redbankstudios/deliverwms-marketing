  atomic transactional safety.

POST 5: SPRINT F — FULL INVOICE LIFECYCLE WITH THREE-LAYER DEFENSE
        (May 4, 2026)
================================================================

URL: https://deliverwms.com/blog/full-revenue-lifecycle-three-layer-defense

KEY POINTS:
- Invoices on Deliver WMS live in one of 8 states: Draft, Sent, Paid,
  Overdue, Disputed, Resolved, Void, Cancelled. Each transition captured
  with who did it, when, and reason if applicable.
- Three-layer defense: UI prevents wrong actions from being clickable, API
  rejects invalid actions, database constraints reject inconsistent states.
- Sent invoices can't be silently changed. Once a line is on a bill that's
  gone to a client, it's frozen. Reissue or void — both preserve the
  original.
- Disputes don't disappear into ambiguity. Client disputes a line, requires
  a reason. Disposition (original stands / reissue / void) captured with
  another reason.
- Paid means paid. The system rejects any invalid combination — like an
  invoice marked paid with no payment date.
- Cancelled is for drafts that never went out. Void is for issued invoices
  that need retraction with a written reason. Tracked separately.
- AR Aging benefit: disputed invoices live in their own column, not in
  90+ bucket. Chase the right accounts.
- Audit prep doesn't take a project. History table answers compliance and
  audit questions in queries instead of PDF reconstructions.

POST 8: INVENTORY YOU CAN TRUST (April 26, 2026)
================================================================

URL: https://deliverwms.com/blog/integrity-sweep-why-we-built-the-audit-first

KEY POINTS:
- Three causes account for almost every inventory discrepancy:
  1. Cycle count entries that never make it to the ledger (no who/when/why)
  2. Status updates that skip the stock movement (status field says one
     thing, stock ledger says another)
  3. Silent backfills (developer "fixes" by editing inventory directly,
     original problem hidden by the fix)
- Most WMS vendors don't surface these until you trip over them. Deliver
  WMS surfaces them the day they happen.
- Daily reconciliation check: compares three independent records (immutable
  movement history, per-item quantity, per-bin balance). Any drift
  surfaces as an exception you see the next morning.
- On-demand integrity self-test: same checks, runnable any time. Useful
  before month-end close.
- Protected stock ledger: every movement captured as immutable entry.
  Adjustments are additional entries, not deletions. Full who/when/why
  record.
- Quarantine for stranger signups: anyone reaching signup without valid
  invitation lands in quarantined state until cleared.
- Result for operators: when dashboard says 412 units, you have 412 units.
  If system and reality disagree, the system tells you before a picker
  finds out the hard way.

POST 9 (FEATURED BENCHMARK): WHY MONTH-END BILLING EATS YOUR WEEK
        (May 26, 2026)
================================================================

URL: https://deliverwms.com/blog/manual-billing-reconciliation-cost-benchmark

KEY POINTS:
- Most 3PL operators underestimate what month-end billing reconciliation
  costs them — finance person hours are obvious, but operations manager
  hours pulling data, dispute conversations that drag for weeks, longer
  receivables cycles, and accounts that quietly leave because invoices
  aren't trustworthy are all hidden costs.
- Where the time goes in manual reconciliation: pulling source data
  (~40%), joining and validating, generating invoices (smallest piece),
  handling disputes (long tail), adding late-arriving adjustments.
- Improving the spreadsheet doesn't change the shape of the problem. The
  invoice is being reconstructed at month-end from data not designed to
  produce invoices.
- The structural fix: capture every billable event at the moment it
  happens. Invoice already assembled by month-end. Reconciliation is a
  one-hour variance review.
- Three short-term improvements operators can make this week:
  1. Settle on rate cards in writing — every active client has a current
     version-dated rate card
  2. Track storage daily, not monthly — daily snapshot of pallet positions
     per client takes minutes
  3. Add a dispute log — track every disputed line item, the client, the
     resolution, and how long it took. Most operators discover the same 5
     line items get disputed every month
- The longer-term answer: a WMS that captures billable events at the moment
  of action (which is what Deliver WMS does).
