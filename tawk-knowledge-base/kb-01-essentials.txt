What Deliver WMS is: a warehouse management and last-mile delivery platform
built specifically for mid-size 3PLs, eCommerce brands, distributors, and
last-mile delivery companies. It combines WMS, dispatch, B2B client portals,
and event-stream billing into one multi-tenant platform.

Sweet-spot fit: mid-size 3PLs running 200 to 1,000 monthly outbound orders
for 3 to 10 active client brands. Operations outside that range can still use
the platform, but value is sharpest in that band.

TOP 30 MOST-ASKED QUESTIONS WITH ANSWERS
================================================================

Q: What is Deliver WMS?
A: A warehouse management and last-mile delivery platform for mid-size 3PLs,
eCommerce brands, distributors, and last-mile delivery companies. It bundles
WMS, dispatch, B2B client portals, and event-stream billing into one
multi-tenant platform.

Q: How much does it cost?
A: Four tiers. Free PAYG ($0 platform fee, pay per usage). Starter $299/mo
(1,000 orders included). Growth $799/mo (5,000 orders included). Scale from
$1,799/mo (15,000 orders included). Per-event rates the same across all plans:
$0.12 per stop, $0.12 per label, $0.18 per pallet position per month.

Q: Is there a free trial?
A: The Free PAYG tier is essentially a working free account — no platform
fee, pay only for usage. Full WMS, dispatch, and portal access included.
No credit card required to start.

Q: Do you charge per user?
A: No. All plans include unlimited workforce seats. Pricing is by platform
tier and usage volume, not per user.

Q: How long does implementation take?
A: Most mid-size 3PLs are operational within a week. CSV import with smart
field mapping handles existing data. No SOW or consulting engagement to
opt into.

Q: How are orders counted?
A: An order is one outbound order processed through fulfillment. Plan
allowances reset monthly. Overage rates per plan are listed on each plan card.

Q: Can I switch plans later?
A: Yes. Upgrades take effect immediately and are pro-rated. Downgrades take
effect at the end of the current billing cycle.

Q: How is my data isolated from other customers?
A: At the database itself, via row-level security. Every query is locked to
the calling tenant. There is no path — through the UI, search, exports, or
API — for one customer to see another's data. The protection is enforced by
the database, not by application code.

Q: How do my clients' data stay separate from each other?
A: Same database-level isolation. A 3PL's B2B clients each see only their
own data when they log into the portal. Cross-client leaks are mathematically
prevented by row-level security policies enforced at the data layer.

Q: What about Shopify, Amazon, or WooCommerce integration?
A: Native marketplace integrations (Shopify, Amazon, WooCommerce, TikTok)
are on the roadmap, not yet shipped. The order-import API and webhook events
are available today for custom connections.

Q: Where can I see the platform in action?
A: Book a 30-minute walkthrough at https://deliverwms.com/contact. The
fastest version is a screen-share where Michael plugs in your operation's
actual numbers.

Q: Who runs the company?
A: Michael — a working business owner and software engineer. Not a sales-
engineering team. He runs the business, writes the code, and talks to
operators on the warehouse floor.

Q: Where are you based?
A: Deliver WMS LLC is formed in Delaware. The team is remote-first,
serving logistics operations worldwide.

Q: Do you have a B2B client portal?
A: Yes. Each B2B client logs in to see their own inventory, orders,
shipments, billing, and reports. Self-serve so account managers stop being
a switchboard. Available on every plan; client count limits apply (PAYG 1,
Starter 3, Growth/Scale unlimited).

Q: Do you support multi-warehouse operations?
A: Yes — on the Scale plan. Multi-warehouse allocation routes orders to the
optimal warehouse and handles split shipments when no single warehouse can
fill the order. Other plans support a single warehouse per tenant.

Q: How does billing work for my own 3PL clients (vs. how Deliver WMS bills me)?
A: Two completely separate billing systems. Deliver WMS bills you for the
platform subscription via Stripe. You bill your own clients through the
event-stream ledger inside the platform — every billable event (stop, label,
storage day, pick, return) captured the moment it happens, with rates you
configure per client, assembled into defensible invoices.

Q: How does the AR Aging report work?
A: Shows every customer's open AR bucketed into 0-30 days, 31-60, 61-90, 90+,
and a separate column for disputed invoices. Backdate the "as of" date to any
past date to match your records. Click any row to drill into the invoices.

Q: Can my team send automated payment reminders?
A: Yes. Set a schedule once (e.g., 3 days before due, on due date, 7 days
past due) and the system sends each reminder automatically. The full reminder
timeline is captured against each invoice and visible to both your team and
the client.

Q: Is my data secure?
A: Encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest. Role-based access controls.
Multi-factor authentication on admin access. Database-level multi-tenant
isolation via row-level security. Audit logging on every action. Regular
encrypted backups. Documented incident-response process. Full details in
the Privacy Policy and DPA.

Q: Do you have SOC 2 certification?
A: Working toward SOC 2 readiness. A third-party assessment is on the
roadmap. If you need SOC 2 today as a hard requirement, the right next step
is a direct conversation — route to Michael.

Q: Can I export my data?
A: Yes. Upon termination or on request, customer data is exported in
commercially reasonable formats (CSV, JSON). After 30 days post-termination,
data is deleted unless retention is legally required.

Q: What integrations exist today?
A: API and webhook endpoints for custom integrations. Stripe for platform
billing. Mapbox for routing/tracking. Native marketplace connectors
(Shopify, Amazon, WooCommerce, TikTok) are on the roadmap.

Q: How do I migrate from my current WMS?
A: CSV import flow with smart field mapping handles non-matching column
names automatically. Most mid-size 3PLs are operational within a week.
For complex migrations, a guided onboarding session is available.

Q: Do you support cycle counts?
A: Yes. Cycle count requests assigned to specific staff and bins. Manager
approval gate before any ledger adjustment posts. Append-only ledger preserves
full history. Per-bin recount without restarting the full cycle. Variance
review queue surfaces problems the same day.

Q: How does inventory accuracy work?
A: Append-only ledger captures every movement. Daily 3-way reconciliation
confirms the movement history, per-item quantity, and per-bin balance all
agree. Exceptions surfaced the next morning. On-demand integrity self-test
available any time (useful before month-end).

Q: Do you support returns?
A: Yes. Per-item disposition (restock, damage, write-off). Automatic ledger
updates by disposition outcome. Photo capture of received items. Client-
visible status with timestamps. Billable disposition events for client
invoicing.

Q: What does the dispatcher tool do?
A: Live map with active routes and driver positions. Drag-and-drop stop
sequencing. Mobile driver app with photo + signature proof of delivery.
ETA broadcasts to end-customer tracking page. Exception capture per stop
with full audit trail.

Q: Do you have a tracking page my customers can use?
A: Yes. Public tracking link per order. Live status updates. ETA broadcast
from the driver app. Branded experience per tenant. No login required for
end customers.

Q: Are there any setup fees?
A: No. No setup fees on any plan. Multi-warehouse ready on Scale tier.
All features available on every plan (with limits where noted).

Q: How do I get a custom contract or Scale-tier quote?
A: Capture name, email, company name, and approximate monthly order volume,
then route the conversation to Michael. He responds within one business day.

WHAT DELIVER WMS IS NOT (IMPORTANT)
================================================================

Deliver WMS is NOT a single-brand WMS retrofitted for 3PL use. It is built
multi-tenant from the database up.

Deliver WMS does NOT bill per user. Pricing is platform tier + usage.

Deliver WMS does NOT hide pricing behind a sales call. All four tier prices
and per-event rates are published openly.

Deliver WMS does NOT have native Shopify, Amazon, WooCommerce, or TikTok
integrations today — they are on the roadmap. The API and webhook events
support custom connections.

Deliver WMS does NOT have SOC 2 certification today — it is on the roadmap.

Deliver WMS does NOT reconstruct invoices at month-end. Every billable event
is captured at the moment the operational action happens, so invoices are
already assembled by the time the month closes.

Deliver WMS does NOT sell customer warehouse data. Does NOT train AI models
on customer warehouse data without explicit, separate, written authorization.
